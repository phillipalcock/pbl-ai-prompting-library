## Mission Overview

Students investigate water quality in their local watershed by collecting and analyzing data from multiple sites. They research how different land uses (residential, agricultural, industrial, recreational) affect water quality and present evidence-based recommendations to a panel acting as the city environmental board.

    ### Four-Tier Learning Goals

    **Tier 1: Content Knowledge**
    - Students will identify the key pollutants that affect freshwater ecosystems and their sources.
    - Students will explain how nutrient levels, pH, dissolved oxygen, and turbidity indicate water health.
    - Students will describe how different land uses contribute to watershed pollution.

    **Tier 2: Skills and Processes**
    - Students will collect water quality data using standardized testing protocols.
    - Students will analyze data sets to identify patterns across sampling sites.
    - Students will create data visualizations (charts, graphs, maps) that communicate findings.

    **Tier 3: Critical Thinking**
    - Students will evaluate the relationship between land use patterns and water quality indicators.
    - Students will construct evidence-based arguments about which land uses pose the greatest risk.
    - Students will propose mitigation strategies supported by their data and research.

    **Tier 4: Communication and Collaboration**
    - Students will present findings to a panel using professional data visualizations.
    - Students will write a technical summary accessible to non-scientist community members.
    - Students will collaborate effectively within assigned roles to produce a cohesive final product.

    ### Assessment Strategy

    **Checkpoints:**

    | Checkpoint | Timing | Deliverable | Weight |
    |-----------|--------|-------------|--------|
    | Research Plan | End of Day 3 | Testing protocol + site map | 10% |
    | Data Collection | End of Week 1 | Completed data spreadsheet | 15% |
    | Analysis Draft | Mid Week 2 | Data visualizations + preliminary findings | 20% |
    | Report Draft | End of Week 2 | Full draft for peer review | 15% |
    | Final Presentation | End of Week 3 | Panel presentation + final report | 40% |

    **Rubric Criteria (Final Product):**

    | Criterion | Exceeds | Meets | Approaching | Beginning |
    |-----------|---------|-------|-------------|-----------|
    | Data Quality | Comprehensive data from all sites with quality controls | Complete data from all sites | Partial data or missing sites | Incomplete or unreliable data |
    | Analysis | Identifies complex patterns with statistical support | Identifies key patterns with evidence | Identifies some patterns | Minimal analysis attempted |
    | Argument | Nuanced recommendations with counterargument consideration | Clear recommendations supported by evidence | Recommendations present but weakly supported | Recommendations not evidence-based |
    | Communication | Polished, audience-appropriate for multiple stakeholders | Clear and appropriate for the target audience | Understandable but not audience-tailored | Unclear or inappropriate for audience |
    | Collaboration | Exceeds role expectations, supports teammates proactively | Fulfills all role responsibilities | Completes most role responsibilities | Does not fulfill role requirements |

- CCSS.ELA W.7.1: Write arguments to support claims with evidence
- CCSS.ELA SL.7.4: Present claims and findings with relevant evidence
