    ### Learning Goals

    **Tier 1: Content Knowledge**
    - Students will identify the structural elements common to myths across cultures (hero's journey, origin stories, moral tales).
    - Students will explain how historical and social context shapes the themes and characters of myths.
    - Students will define key literary terms: archetype, allegory, motif, symbolism, and narrative structure.

    **Tier 2: Skills and Processes**
    - Students will conduct close reading analysis of mythological texts, identifying literary devices and cultural values.
    - Students will gather primary source material (interviews, media analysis) about modern community narratives.
    - Students will compare texts across time periods using structured analytical frameworks.

    **Tier 3: Critical Thinking**
    - Students will evaluate the extent to which ancient myths and modern narratives serve parallel cultural functions.
    - Students will analyze how power, identity, and social values are embedded in the stories cultures tell about themselves.
    - Students will construct an original argument about the role of myth-making in contemporary society.

    **Tier 4: Communication and Collaboration**
    - Students will create a multimedia exhibition piece (written, visual, or audio) that presents their analysis.
    - Students will write an analytical essay connecting ancient and modern mythologies.
    - Students will present their work to a public audience with clear, engaging delivery.

    ### Assessment Strategy

    **Checkpoints:**

    | Checkpoint | Timing | Deliverable | Weight |
    |-----------|--------|-------------|--------|
    | Text Selection | End of Week 1 | Annotated myth selections + rationale | 10% |
    | Research + Interviews | End of Week 2 | Research notes, interview transcripts, media analysis | 15% |
    | Comparative Analysis | Mid Week 3 | Comparison charts + draft thesis | 20% |
    | Exhibition Draft | End of Week 3 | Draft exhibition piece + essay outline | 15% |
    | Final Exhibition | End of Week 4 | Complete exhibition + analytical essay | 40% |

    **Rubric Criteria (Final Product):**

    | Criterion | Exceeds | Meets | Approaching | Beginning |
    |-----------|---------|-------|-------------|-----------|
    | Literary Analysis | Sophisticated analysis with nuanced connections across multiple texts | Thorough analysis identifying key devices and themes | Basic analysis with some connections | Superficial or inaccurate analysis |
    | Cultural Argument | Original thesis with multi-layered evidence from ancient and modern sources | Clear thesis supported by evidence from both time periods | Thesis present but unevenly supported | No clear argument or unsupported claims |
    | Modern Connection | Insightful parallels between myth and contemporary narratives with original examples | Clear connections between ancient and modern myth-making | Some connections attempted | No meaningful modern connections |
    | Exhibition Quality | Polished, engaging multimedia piece with professional presentation | Complete multimedia piece with clear presentation | Partially complete or inconsistent quality | Incomplete or unclear exhibition piece |
    | Written Expression | Exceptional prose with sophisticated vocabulary and flawless mechanics | Clear, well-organized writing with appropriate style | Understandable but with organizational or mechanical issues | Unclear writing with significant errors |
