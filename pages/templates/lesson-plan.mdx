## What's Included

This template guides you through planning a single lesson:

1. **Lesson Identity** — Title, subject, grade, date, and duration
2. **Standards and Objectives** — Targeted standards and measurable learning objectives
3. **Materials and Preparation** — Resources, technology, and advance preparation needed
4. **Lesson Sequence** — Structured flow from opening to closure
5. **Assessment** — How you will check for understanding
6. **Differentiation Notes** — Supports, modifications, and extensions
7. **Reflection** — Post-lesson notes on what worked and what to adjust

## Lesson Sequence Structure

The template uses a gradual release model:

- **Opening** — Engage students, activate prior knowledge, and share the learning objective
- **Direct Instruction** — Introduce new content through explanation, modeling, or demonstration
- **Guided Practice** — Students practice with teacher support and feedback
- **Independent Practice** — Students apply learning independently or in small groups
- **Closure** — Summarize key takeaways, assess understanding, and preview next steps

## How to Use

1. Make a copy of the template document
2. Fill in the lesson identity and select your target standards
3. Write 2-4 measurable learning objectives
4. Plan your lesson sequence using the gradual release structure
5. Identify assessment strategies for checking understanding
6. Add differentiation notes for diverse learners
7. After teaching, complete the reflection section to inform future planning

<Callout type="info" title="Tip">
Use AI to generate multiple options for each section — for example, three different opening hooks or several exit ticket questions — then select the best fit for your students.
</Callout>
